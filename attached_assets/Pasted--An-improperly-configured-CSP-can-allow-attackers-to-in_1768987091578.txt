Examples of insecure CSP headers:
*	`Content-Security-Policy: default-src *;` (Allows loading resources from any origin)
*	`Content-Security-Policy: script-src 'unsafe-inline';` (Allows execution of inline scripts)
*	`Content-Security-Policy: script-src 'unsafe-eval';` (Allows the use of eval() and similar functions)
*	`Content-Security-Policy: ;` (Missing CSP header)



A strong CSP should:
*	Explicitly allow only trusted sources for scripts and other resources.
*	Avoid 'unsafe-inline' and 'unsafe-eval' where possible.
*	Use nonces or hashes for scripts and styles.
*	Restrict the 'default-src' directive.
*	Consider using 'strict-dynamic' for modern browsers.

default-src 'none';
